Can you give me a 20 holiday plan with 2 10 day legs Japan then Vietnam. Fly out from Sydney to Tokyo, up through to Kyoto, fly out to Vietnam for resort holiday then fly back to Sydney

Here is a comprehensive 20-day multi-leg itinerary designed for a balance of vibrant city exploration in Japan and a relaxing beach resort getaway in Vietnam.

Leg 1: Japan (Days 1–10)
This leg takes you from the modern neon-lit districts of Tokyo down to the historic temples of Kyoto via the Shinkansen (bullet train).

Day 1: Arrival in Tokyo


Sydney Airport
3.8

✈️ International airport

Depart from Sydney Airport and arrive at Tokyo Haneda or Narita Airport.



Tokyo

Take a limousine bus or express train to your hotel. Spend your evening resetting and getting a feel for Tokyo with a relaxed walk and dinner in your local neighborhood (such as Shinjuku or Shibuya).

Day 2: Modern Tokyo Icons
Spend the morning exploring the famous Shibuya Crossing and checking out the panoramic views from Shibuya Sky.

Walk down the boutique-lined streets of Omotesando and explore Takeshita Street in Harajuku.

Finish the evening with dinner at a lively izakaya in Shinjuku's Omoide Yokocho.

Day 3: Historic Asakusa & Electric Akihabara
Visit Senso-ji, Tokyo’s oldest Buddhist temple, located in the historic Asakusa district.

Dive into the futuristic subculture of Akihabara for electronics, retro gaming, and anime culture.

Catch a late-afternoon view from the Tokyo Skytree.

Day 4: Seafood & Digital Art
Head down to Tsukiji Outer Market for a breakfast of fresh sushi, wagyu skewers, and street food classics.

Spend the afternoon immersed in the digital light installations at teamLab Planets in Toyosu.

Enjoy a sunset waterfront walk in Odaiba looking over the Rainbow Bridge.

Day 5: Day Trip to Mt. Fuji (Hakone)
Take a day trip out of the city to Hakone using the Romancecar train.

Ride the Hakone Ropeway, cruise across Lake Ashi on a pirate ship, and catch stunning views of Mount Fuji if weather permits.

Unwind in a traditional Japanese open-air hot spring (onsen) before returning to Tokyo.

Day 6: Bullet Train to Kyoto


Kyoto

Check out of your hotel and board the Shinkansen from Tokyo to Kyoto (approx. 2.15 hours).

Drop your bags at your accommodation and spend the afternoon wandering through the historic wooden lanes of Gion, keeping an eye out for geishas en route to evening appointments.

Day 7: Bamboo Groves & Golden Pavilions
Head out early to Arashiyama to walk through the towering Bamboo Grove before the crowds arrive.

Visit the nearby Tenryu-ji Temple and its stunning Zen garden.

Take a short train ride to see Kinkaku-ji (The Golden Pavilion), which reflects beautifully over its surrounding pond.

Day 8: Torii Gates & Historic Streets
Wake up early to hike through the thousands of vibrant vermilion gates at Fushimi Inari Shrine.

In the afternoon, walk up the atmospheric slope of Ninenzaka and Sannenzaka streets leading to Kiyomizu-dera Temple, which offers sweeping views over Kyoto.

Day 9: Day Trip to Nara
Take a 45-minute train south to Nara, Japan's first permanent capital.

Bow to the free-roaming deer in Nara Park and visit Todai-ji Temple, home to one of Japan's largest bronze Buddha statues.

Head back to Kyoto for a final dinner featuring traditional Kaiseki cuisine or local Kyoto-style ramen.

Day 10: Kyoto to Vietnam via Osaka
Take the Haruka Express from Kyoto directly to Kansai International Airport (KIX) in Osaka.

Board your flight to Central Vietnam (Da Nang International Airport). Most direct flights or short transfers arrive by late afternoon or evening. Check into your luxury beach resort to begin Leg 2.

Leg 2: Vietnam Resort Holiday (Days 11–20)
For the perfect resort leg, Da Nang/Hoi An is ideal because it features world-class beachfront properties, an accessible international airport, and beautiful cultural day trips.

Day 11: Rest & Resort Relaxation
Dedicate your first full day in Vietnam entirely to slowing down.

Sleep in, enjoy a beachfront tropical breakfast, lounge by the infinity pool, and book a traditional Vietnamese hot stone massage at your resort spa.

Day 12: Hoi An Ancient Town
Spend the morning enjoying the beach.

In the mid-afternoon, take a short 20-minute shuttle to the UNESCO-listed Hoi An Ancient Town. Explore the yellow-walled merchant houses, tailor shops, and the Japanese Covered Bridge.

Stay past sunset to watch the entire town light up with thousands of colorful silk lanterns and take a short boat ride down the Thu Bon River.

Day 13: Marble Mountains & Seafood Feast
Take a short morning trip to the Marble Mountains—a cluster of five limestone hills packed with hidden caves, Buddhist sanctuaries, and panoramic coastal viewpoints.

Head back to the coast for a late lunch at a local beachfront shack to feast on fresh garlic butter prawns, clams, and grilled fish.

Day 14: Day Trip to Ba Na Hills
Head inland to the Ba Na Hills mountain resort. Ride one of the world's longest single-track cable cars up through the clouds.

Walk along the iconic Golden Bridge, held up by two giant stone hands, and explore the replica French Village at the peak.

Day 15: Beachfront Leisure & Water Sports
Spend a completely unscheduled day at the resort.

Take advantage of the resort's water sports like paddleboarding or kayaking, or simply read a book on the private white-sand beach.

Day 16: My Son Sanctuary Culture Day
Take a half-day morning tour to My Son Sanctuary, a cluster of abandoned and partially ruined Hindu temples constructed between the 4th and 14th centuries by the Champa kings.

Return to the resort for an afternoon poolside cocktail and an evening beach barbecue.

Day 17: Explore Da Nang City
Venture into Da Nang city center. Visit the Cham Museum of Sculpture and stroll along the Han River waterfront.

Make sure you are near the river around 9:00 PM if it's a weekend to watch the massive Dragon Bridge breathe actual fire and water.

Day 18: Son Tra Peninsula (Monkey Mountain)
Take a scenic coastal drive up the Son Tra Peninsula. Visit the Linh Ung Pagoda, which features a massive 67-meter-tall Lady Buddha statue looking out over the bay.

Keep an eye out for the rare red-shanked douc langurs that inhabit the nature reserve.

Day 19: Final Resort Indulgence
Spend your final full day in paradise enjoying the premium amenities of your resort. Take a final swim, enjoy a beachfront dinner, and pack your bags for the journey home.

Day 20: Departure to Sydney
Check out of your resort and transfer to Da Nang International Airport.

Catch your flight back to Sydney (typically connecting via Ho Chi Minh City, Hanoi, or Singapore), arriving back in Australia refreshed from your holiday.

Can you make Japan 3 cities?

Here is the updated 20-day multi-leg itinerary. The first leg has been adjusted to fit three distinct cities in Japan—Tokyo, Kyoto, and Osaka—offering a perfect mix of ultra-modern metropolis, traditional culture, and incredible regional street food before you fly out to Vietnam.

Leg 1: Japan (Days 1–10) — 3 Cities
Day 1: Arrival in Tokyo


Sydney Airport
3.8

✈️ International airport

Depart from Sydney Airport and arrive at Tokyo.

Take the express train or airport limousine bus straight to your hotel. Spend a relaxed evening walking around your local neighborhood to adjust to the new time zone.

Day 2: Highlights of Modern Tokyo
Start early at Shibuya Crossing, then take in the views from Shibuya Sky.

Walk through the historic forested grounds of Meiji Jingu Shrine, followed by a stroll through Harajuku and the upscale boutique street of Omotesando.

Spend the evening exploring the neon corridors and izakayas of Shinjuku.

Day 3: Historic Asakusa & Immersive Art
Head to Asakusa to visit Senso-ji, Tokyo's oldest temple, and browse the traditional stalls along Nakamise Street.

In the afternoon, dive into the spectacular digital light installations at teamLab Planets in Toyosu.

Finish the day watching the sunset over the waterfront in Odaiba.

Day 4: Shinkansen to Kyoto


Kyoto

Check out and board the Shinkansen (bullet train) from Tokyo to Kyoto (approx. 2 hours 15 minutes).

Drop your bags at your hotel and spend the late afternoon wandering through the historic, lantern-lit streets of the Gion district and Ninenzaka slope.

Day 5: Bamboo Forests & Golden Pavilions
Head out early to the Arashiyama Bamboo Grove to beat the mid-day crowds, visiting the serene Tenryu-ji Temple grounds right alongside it.

In the afternoon, travel to the northern side of the city to witness the stunning gold-leaf architecture of Kinkaku-ji (The Golden Pavilion).

Day 6: Fushimi Inari & Transit to Osaka
Wake up early to hike through the thousands of vermilion torii gates at Fushimi Inari Shrine.



Osaka

Return to your hotel, grab your bags, and take a quick 30-minute local train line down to Osaka—your third city.

Check-in and head straight to Dotonbori at night to experience the massive neon signs, bustling canals, and incredible street food atmosphere.

Day 7: Osaka History & Modern Views
Spend the morning exploring the expansive grounds and impressive architecture of Osaka Castle.

Head over to the retro Shinsekai neighborhood for lunch to try Kushikatsu (skewered deep-fried meats and vegetables).

In the evening, get a spectacular panoramic view of the metropolitan sprawl from the Umeda Sky Building.

Day 8: Day Trip to Nara
Take a convenient 40-minute train ride from Osaka to Nara Park.

Spend the day bowing to the famous free-roaming deer and marveling at the giant bronze Buddha housed inside Todai-ji Temple.

Head back to Osaka for a dinner of local culinary specialties like Okonomiyaki (savory cabbage pancakes) and Takoyaki (octopus balls).

Day 9: Flexible Day (Universal Studios or Himeji Castle)
Option A: Spend a thrilling day at Universal Studios Japan to experience Super Nintendo World and the Wizarding World of Harry Potter.

Option B: Take a short Shinkansen ride to see Himeji Castle, widely considered Japan's most spectacular and pristine original samurai castle.

Day 10: Osaka to Central Vietnam
Take the express train directly to Osaka Kansai International Airport (KIX).

Board your flight to Da Nang International Airport in Central Vietnam. Transfer to your coastal beach resort, unpack, and settle in for your relaxing second leg.

Leg 2: Vietnam Resort Holiday (Days 11–20)
Day 11: Dedicated Resort Decompression
Sleep in late, enjoy a long beachfront breakfast, and spend the day lounging poolside or reading on the private sand. Treat yourself to a traditional massage at the resort spa.

Day 12: Hoi An Lantern Town
Enjoy a slow morning at the resort. In the afternoon, take a quick shuttle to the historic merchant town of Hoi An Ancient Town.

Explore the preserved yellow buildings and the Japanese Covered Bridge, then watch the town illuminate with thousands of silk lanterns at dusk.

Day 13: Marble Mountains & Local Seafood
Take a short morning excursion to the Marble Mountains to explore the limestone caves and Buddhist grottoes carved into the cliffs.

Head back to the coast to feast on fresh, locally caught seafood right by the water.

Day 14: Ba Na Hills & The Golden Bridge
Head up into the mountains via a scenic cable car ride to Ba Na Hills. Walk across the iconic Golden Bridge, held up by giant stone hands, and enjoy the cooler alpine air.

Day 15: Beachfront Leisure & Water Sports
Enjoy an entirely unscripted resort day. Try some water sports like paddleboarding or kayaking, or simply enjoy premium room service by the ocean.

Day 16: Ancient Ruins of My Son Sanctuary
Take a half-day morning tour to the UNESCO-listed My Son Sanctuary to admire the striking brick temple ruins built by the ancient Champa civilization. Return for a relaxed afternoon by the pool.

Day 17: Highlights of Da Nang City
Venture into Da Nang city center to visit local markets and coastal pagodas. If it's a weekend evening, head down to the riverfront to watch the massive Dragon Bridge blast fire and water at 9:00 PM.

Day 18: Son Tra Peninsula Exploration
Drive up the winding roads of the Son Tra Peninsula to see the majestic 67-meter-tall Lady Buddha statue at Linh Ung Pagoda, taking in views of the entire Da Nang bay.

Day 19: Final Resort Indulgence
Spend your final full day completely winding down at your resort. Enjoy a special farewell dinner on the beach to mark the end of the trip.

Day 20: Departure to Sydney
Check out of your resort, transfer to the airport, and board your flight back home to Sydney, completely refreshed from the balance of fast-paced cities and slow-paced beaches.
